# Hi, I'm Mohsen Khalili (Dermani)

Economics M.A. and research assistant working in **empirical and labour economics**. I turn large, messy public micro-datasets into clean, reproducible panels and credible estimates — and I care as much about honest identification as about the headline result.

## What I do

- **Applied microeconomics & labour economics** — employment/unemployment/inactivity transitions, income and wealth dynamics over the life cycle.
- **Panel-data engineering** — harmonising U.S. survey micro-data (PSID, NLSY79/97, CPS, HMDA) into analysis-ready panels.
- **Causal inference** — two-way fixed effects, event studies, shift-share designs, and careful robustness checks.
- **Reproducible research** — documented, end-to-end pipelines with publication-quality figures and LaTeX write-ups.

## Tech & tools

`Stata` · `R` · `Python` · `Julia` · `LaTeX` · `Git`

## Featured projects

- **[psid-labor-transitions](https://github.com/mkhdermani/psid-labor-transitions)** — cross-dataset study of labour-market transitions and wealth dynamics, built from PSID, NLSY and CPS and validated against FRED and published benchmarks.
- **[housing-credit-entrepreneurship](https://github.com/mkhdermani/housing-credit-entrepreneurship)** — county-level panel study testing whether expanding federal mortgage-credit limits stimulate U.S. entrepreneurship, with a full identification ladder and an honest null result.
